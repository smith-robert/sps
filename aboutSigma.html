UCF CHAPTER OF SIGMA PI SIGMA (ΣΠΣ)

Constitution Guidelines and Bylaws

ARTICLE I: NAME

The name of this organization shall be “Chapter of the Sigma Pi Sigma at the University of Central Florida”.

ARTICLE II: PREAMBLE

The purpose of this Chapter shall be:

  • to serve as a means of awarding distinction to students having high scholarship and promise of achievement in physics
  • to promote student interest in research and the advanced study of the science
  • to encourage a professional spirit and friendship among those who have displayed marked ability in physics
  • to popularize interest in physics in the general college public.
ARTICLE III: ADMINISTRATION

The Chapter is run by a committee made of three members. The members of the committee are selected from among those Faculty of the Physics Department who have strong interest in the Undergraduate Curriculum and Undergraduate Physics Education. One of the three members shall be the advisor of the SPS UCF Chapter unless he elects not to do so. The committee will select new members according to the eligibility criteria. If it decides, the committee may appoint three officers (a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer) to assist with the daily administration of the Chapter.

ARTICLE IV: SELECTION OF NEW MEMBERS
A. Undergraduate Students

Any student enrolled at UCF may request membership in the Chapter. The committee may also nominate students to become members. In all such cases, a positive decision will be made if the student satisfies ALL of the following criteria:

  • The student has junior status in any major.
  • The student has taken at least three courses (that can be counted for the physics major) from the UCF Physics Department course offerings.
  • The student has at least 9 credit hours at 3000-level in physics courses taken at UCF or officially transferred from another university.
  • The student has an overall minimum GPA of 3.0 and a Physics minimum GPA of 3.5.
B. Graduate Students

Any graduate student enrolled at UCF (from the Department of Physics or other Departments) may request membership in the Chapter. The Committee may also nominate students to become members. In all such cases, a positive decision will be made if the student satisfies the following criteria:

  • The student has at least 12 credit hours of core physics graduate courses. Courses transferred officially from other universities as ‘core courses’ count in the calculation of credit hours.
  • The student has a minimum GPA of 3.75 calculated from the core physics grad- uate courses.
  • Graduate research and independent study may not be counted in the calculation of credit hours or GPA.
C. Transfer Members

Members of other Sigma Pi Sigma Chapters can transfer their membership to the UCF Chapter with the Committee’s approval.

D. Honorary Members

The Committee can elect new honorary members at any time.

E. Other Regular Members

The Committee can invite new regular members at any time. For A and B an official transcript must be submitted after entry of the preceding term’s grades and no later than one month prior to the announced date of the Induction Ceremony.

ARTICLE V: MEMBERSHIP

Membership is limited to all students, faculty, and other professionals who have paid fees and are in good standing with the National Chapter. No discrimination shall be made on the basis of gender, race, color, creed, age, religion, disability, national origin, marital status, sexual orientation/preference, parental status or veteran’s status. No hazing or discrimination will be used as a condition of membership in this organization.

ARTICLE VI: ACTIVITIES

Sigma Pi Sigma is an honor society existing within the Society of Physics Students. Members are expected to be active in SPS throughout the year with special emphasis on the promotion of extracurricular activities of the Physics Department.

ARTICLE VII: EXTERNAL AFFILIATIONS

This is a local chapter of the National Sigma Pi Sigma. Items not covered in this constitution shall be governed by the National Society’s constitution where it does not conflict with the local chapter’s.

ARTICLE VIII: PUBLICATIONS

This local chapter shall forward an annual report to the national chapter as required.

ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS

Amendments shall conform to the regulations established by the national constitution of the Sigma Pi Sigma or agree with the spirit set forth by the same.

HISTORY OF THE CONSTITUTION

This constitution was written and approved on Monday, November 22, 2004.

It was revised on Thursday, September 29, 2004.

It was further revised on Wednesday, January 31, 2007.
